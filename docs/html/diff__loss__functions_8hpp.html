Gradient (first derivative) of Mean Squared Error loss.

\( \displaystyle \nabla L_{MSE} = -\frac{2}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} X^T (y_{i} - \hat{y_{i}}) \), where \( X \) is the features matrix, \( y \) is the target vector, \( \hat{y} \) is the model's predictions vector, \( n \) is the number of predictions.

Laplacian (second derivative) of Log Likelihood loss.

\( \displaystyle \nabla (\nabla L_{LOG}) = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n} X^T \hat{y_{i}} (1 - \hat{y_{i}})^T \), where \( X \) is the features matrix, \( \hat{y} \) is the model's predictions vector, \( n \) is the number of predictions.
